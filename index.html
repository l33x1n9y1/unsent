A safe space for nurses

For the words you never got to send

After a hard shift, some feelings have no destination. After Hours gives them a home — through the quiet, healing act of writing letters that are never sent, but always heard. So that when you walk out those doors, you can leave the weight of the ward behind, and come home to yourself.

How it works

A ritual of release,
built on evidence

01

Write privately

After hours, nurses write a short unsent letter — to a patient, a mistake, a difficult moment. The writing itself is the therapy; it is never sent and never stored with identity.

02

Choose to share

Writers may opt in to share their letter anonymously. A small curatorial team selects letters for the monthly physical installation in nurse rest areas.

03

The Wall appears

Once a month, selected letters are beautifully typeset on card stock and displayed in a physical Wall of Words — transforming private pain into shared humanity.

04

The cycle continues

Nurses see their colleagues' words on the wall. They feel recognised, less alone. The next shift, another letter begins — and the community of care deepens.
